## Founders / origins (the CHI 2001 NIME workshop)

NIME began as a workshop at ACM CHI 2001 (Seattle). The workshop proposal —
Poupyrev, Lyons, Fels & Blaine, *"New Interfaces for Musical Expression,"* CHI'01
Extended Abstracts, pp. 491–492 — carries the four people consistently described
as co-founders. NIME became a standalone conference from 2002.

- **Ivan Poupyrev** — first-listed author of the CHI'01 workshop proposal; then at Sony CSL, now Google ATAP. <http://www.ivanpoupyrev.com/>
- **Michael J. Lyons** (Ritsumeikan University) — co-founder; co-editor of *A NIME Reader*; co-chaired NIME 2004 (Hamamatsu). <https://kasrl.org/>
- **Sidney (Sid) Fels** (University of British Columbia) — co-founder; co-chaired NIME 2005 (Vancouver); directs the HCT Lab. <https://ece.ubc.ca/sid-fels/>
- **Tina "Bean" Blaine** — co-founder; creator of the Jam-O-Drum; co-chaired NIME 2005; later CMU Entertainment Technology Center. <https://www.etc.cmu.edu/blog/author/tblaine/>

## Deep-history figures (threaded through NIME)

- **Marcelo M. Wanderley** (McGill, IDMIL) — most-cited author across NIME's first 20 years; chaired NIME 2003 (Montreal). <https://www.idmil.org/people/marcelo-m-wanderley/>
- **Alexander Refsum Jensenius** (Oslo / RITMO) — co-editor of *A NIME Reader*; chaired NIME 2011 (Oslo); chaired the NIME Steering Committee 2011–2022. <https://www.arj.no/>
- **Atau Tanaka** (Goldsmiths) — helped establish NIME; pioneer of EMG/biosignal performance; chaired NIME 2014 (London). <http://www.ataut.net/> (HTTP only)
- **Joseph A. Paradiso** (MIT Media Lab, Responsive Environments) — sensor-systems pioneer; chaired NIME 2002 (Dublin). <https://www.media.mit.edu/people/joep/overview/>
- **Perry R. Cook** (Princeton, emeritus) — author of the seminal "Principles for Designing Computer Music Controllers" (NIME 2001); co-founder of PLOrk. <https://www.cs.princeton.edu/~prc/>
- **Tod Machover** (MIT Media Lab, Opera of the Future) — hyperinstruments pioneer. <https://www.media.mit.edu/people/tod/overview/>
- **Axel Mulder** — early virtual/immersive-instrument researcher (SFU); I-CubeX / Infusion Systems sensor toolkit widely used at NIME. (Old academic site repurposed; no current personal URL verified.)

## Committees & initiatives (and their leaders)

- **NIME Board** — the governing body (replaced the Steering Committee); selects hosts/chairs and oversees committees. <https://nime.org/board/> — currently incl. Fabio Morreale (President), Juan Martinez Avila (Chair of Committees), Andrew McPherson (Continuity Officer), Stefano Fasciani (Proceedings Officer), Isabela Corintha Almeida (Member-at-large), Courtney N. Reed & Charles Martin (NIME 2026 / 2025 chairs).
- **Diversity at NIME** — inclusive-conference resource and action space; runs mentorship and hosts WiNIME / LATAM activities. <https://diversity.nime.org/>
- **WiNIME (Women in NIME)** — directory of women in NIME for sourcing speakers, committee members and reviewers; led by Anna Xambó. <https://annaxambo.me/directory-of-winime/>
- **Ethics Committee** — maintains the NIME Principles & Code of Practice on Ethical Research. <https://www.nime.org/ethics/>
- **Eco / Environment (ECO_NIME)** — the NIME environmental statement and Eco Wiki; environmental officers John Sullivan, Raul Masu, Adam Pultz Melbye. Wiki: <https://eco.nime.org/> · statement: <https://nime.org/environment/>
- **LATAM NIME (Latin American NIME Network)** — regional community, resources and events; founders incl. Juan Martinez Avila, João Tragtenberg, Filipe Calegario, Isabela Corintha, Adnan Marquez-Borbon, Miguel Ortiz, Hugo Solís and others. <https://latam.nime.org/>

See also [organiser reflections](01-organiser-reflections.html) for first-hand
writing by several of the people above.
