Background reading for **Session 2 — "So, you want to propose a NIME…"**. There
is no single proceedings paper on hosting NIME, so this collects first-hand
reflections, official guidance, and community discussion from previous
organisers. Two of the sources below are by workshop facilitators (Charles
Martin, Fabio Morreale).

## First-hand host retrospectives

- **Charles Martin — "On hosting NIME2025 in Canberra"** (6 Feb 2026). A
  general chair's reflection on what NIME2025 (ANU, Canberra) produced, what was
  achieved, and who made it happen. <https://charlesmartin.au/blog/2026/02/06/on-hosting-nime2025-in-canberra>

## Official guidance for prospective hosts

- **NIME Hosting page** — the concrete scaffold for the "plan a NIME in an hour"
  activity: the required proposal components, review criteria (venue, team,
  theme, diversity, environmental impact, financial feasibility), and current
  regional priorities for bids. <https://nime.org/hosting/>
- **NIME hosting proposal template** — <https://github.com/nime-conference/nime-hosting-proposal-template/>
- **NIME Cookbook** — operational guidance for chairs and committees (some
  sections may be out of date). <https://github.com/NIME-conference/NIME-cookbook>
- **Example NIME 2025 proposal** — a worked example to model against.
  <https://github.com/smcclab/nime2025-proposal-example>

## Community discussion on the future of hosting

- **"Note on future NIMEs"** (NIME Forum) — Fabio Morreale, on behalf of the
  Steering Committee, sets out five requirements for future hosts (motivated
  local team + institutional support; supportive academic/cultural environment;
  accessibility for non-affiliated contributors; financial and ecological
  sustainability, especially travel; recognition of a geographically and
  economically diverse community). The thread also discusses hub-based,
  lower-carbon conference models and their trade-offs (with Alex McLean / yaxu).
  <https://forum.nime.org/t/note-on-future-nimes/344>

## Proceedings, format, and infrastructure

- **Alexander Refsum Jensenius — "Structuring NIME content"** (16 Jul 2021) —
  on organising and archiving conference content across tracks.
  <https://www.arj.no/2021/07/16/nime-content/>
- **Alexander Refsum Jensenius — "Running a hybrid conference"** (27 Jun 2021) —
  practical notes on hybrid format (written about the RPPW workshop, so adjacent
  to NIME rather than about it directly). <https://www.arj.no/2021/06/27/hybrid-conference/>
