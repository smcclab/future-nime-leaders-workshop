## Goals

- Participants sketch a *feasible* NIME proposal using the official template
- Participants confront the realities of hosting (finance, bureaucracy, space, resources)
- Facilitators learn what future hosts could bring and what support they'd need

## Materials

- Printed summary of the hosting proposal template
- Link sheet: proposal template + cookbook (see resources page)
- Optional: example proposal (NIME 2025) for reference
- Sticky notes per participant

## Background reading

- Organiser reflections, official hosting guidance, and the proposal template are collected in `resources/01-organiser-reflections.md`
- Includes host retrospectives (e.g. Charles Martin on NIME2025) and the Steering Committee's "Note on future NIMEs"

## Plan for the session (~60m)

1. Provocation + template/cookbook intro (`lectures/02-propose-a-nime`)
2. Group activity: plan a NIME in an hour
3. Pitch-back + facilitator reality-check

## Activities

### Activity 1: Plan a NIME (small groups)

- Groups fill a cut-down proposal template: theme, venue, dates, budget sketch, committee, accessibility
- Constraint: work from resources you actually have, not a dream conference
- Optional: assign each group a constraint (tight budget, specific region, fixed venue)

### Activity 2: Pitch-back

- 2-min pitch per group
- Facilitators (former chairs) reality-check: misconceptions, hidden support, real constraints

## Facilitator notes

- Prepare 1-2 real budget/logistics anecdotes to ground the discussion
- Balance encouragement (experimental NIMEs welcome) with realities
